# HR Attrition Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition using the IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition dataset.

The dashboard identifies the main factors affecting employee turnover by analyzing employee demographics, job characteristics, work-life balance, overtime, salary, and years at the company.

---

## 🛠️ Tools Used

- Power BI
- Power Query
- DAX

---

## 📊 Dashboard Pages

### 1. HR Attrition Dashboard

Provides an overview of employee attrition including:

- Total Employees
- Total Attrition
- Attrition Rate
- Average Age
- Average Salary
- Attrition Rate by Department
- Attrition Rate by Job Role
- Attrition Rate by Education
- Attrition Rate by Gender
- Attrition Rate by Age Group

---

### 2. Employee Attrition Analysis

Analyzes the factors affecting employee attrition:

- Distance From Home by Job Role and Attrition
- Average Salary by Education and Attrition
- Attrition Rate by OverTime
- Attrition Rate by Environment Satisfaction
- Attrition Rate by Work-Life Balance
- Attrition Rate by Years at Company

---

### 3. Key Insights

Main findings from the analysis:

- Sales Representatives have the highest attrition rate (39.76%).
- Employees working overtime are nearly three times more likely to leave than those who do not.
- Employees with poor work-life balance show the highest attrition.
- Attrition is highest among employees in the early years of employment.
- Employees with lower monthly income generally have higher attrition.
